Empirical Formula

Step one:

Convert percent composition to grams.

Step two:

Multiply the quantities of the elements (in grams)
by their molar masses (in moles per grams).

Step three:

Divide the products (in moles) by the smallest molar mass.

Step four:

Round the products (in moles) to the nearest whole number.

Step five:

Substitute the whole number as the subscript of each element.

Step six:

Write the elements beside each other, and you're done!

Molecular Formula

Step one:

Multiply the subscript of the elements by their molar masses.

Step two:

Add the products.

Step three:

Divide the molar mass of the compound by the sum of the products.

Step four:

Multiply the subscript of the elements by the quotient, and you're done!

Limiting Reactant

The limiting reactant has the smallest moles of an element per
coefficent of an element.

Step one:

Convert the grams of the reactants to moles. Multiply by the molar mass.

Step two:

Convert the reactant unit to the product unit. Multiply the moles of the reactant by the ratio of the coefficient of the reactant to the coefficient of the product.

Step three:

The smallest product is the theoretical yield, and the reactant it came from is the limiting reactant.

Excess Reactant

The excess reactant has the largest moles of an element per
coefficent of an element.

Step one:

Convert the grams of the reactants to moles. Multiply by the molar mass.

Step two:

Convert the reactant unit to the product unit. Multiply the moles of the reactant by the ratio of the coefficient of the reactant to the coefficient of the product.

Step three:

The one that resulted into a larger product is the excess reactant of the chemical reaction.

Theoretical Yield

The theoretical yield is the maximum yield possible for the reactants.

Step one:

Convert the grams of the reactants to moles. Multiply by the molar mass.

Step two:

Convert the reactant unit to the product unit. Multiply the moles of the reactant by the ratio of the coefficient of the reactant to the coefficient of the product.

Step three:

The smallest product is the theoretical yield, and the reactant it came from is the limiting reactant.

Percent Yield

The percent yield is 100% times the actual yield divided by the theoretical yield.

Step one:

Get the theoretical yield.

Step two:

Substitute the theoretical yield and the actual yield in the formula. The actual yield is always smaller than the theoretical yield.

Step three:

Divide the actual yield by the theoretical yield, then multiply by 100%. The answer is the percent yield of the reaction

Quantum Numbers

m

  • Principal Quantum Number
  • The energy level of an electron
  • A natural number (1, 2, 3, ...)
  • The larger the number, the farther away from the nucleus

l

  • Angular Momentum Quantum Number
  • Describes the shape of the orbital
    • When l = 0, s-orbital
    • When l = 1, p-orbital
    • When l = 2, d-orbital
    • When l = 3, f-orbital
  • 0 to (n - 1)

ml

  • Magnetic Quantum Number
  • Determines how many orbitals
    of a type per energy level
  • Describes a specific orbital
    amongst a set
  • -l  ≤  ml  ≤  l

ms

  • Spin Quantum Number
  • +½  or  -½
  • Describes the spin of an electron
  • Spins are denoted as ↑ and ↓
    for +½  and  -½ respectively
